Smart Building Initiative

Objective

To design an indicator to increase awareness of energy levels and consumption in Sutton Hall.

Statement

The purpose behind this status indicator goes beyond visualizing information in a clear or pleasing way but was an attempt to reshape how students interact with buildings.

Embedded in a building’s design are notions about how we are to relate it. Often their design passively consents its users to consume its resources without considering their value or encouraging their stewardship.

Consequently, our relationship is parasitic rather than symbiotic. This project seeks to re-frame the way students relate with their built environment by creating informational and interactive experiences by inviting them to become better caretakers of the buildings they use.

Divergent Thinking

I explored different ways to represent energy taking influence from contemporary and historical references.

Information Design

The information needed to motivate behavior change and consider the limited time students spend in the building.

Ideas Taking Form

Initial ideas relied on using screen interfaces. However, more budget-conscious solutions were available.

Rapid Prototyping

I developed an iterative process to refine a selected design through a series of permutations and material explorations.
