Litter to Life

A proposal for the City of Austin's Watershed Protection Department.

Objective

To define a problem space and propose a solution addressing the social and environmental impacts of dog waste management for Austin’s Watershed Protection Department.

Statement

I sensed an understanding of human behavior and public policy could provide a foundation to address the problem. My research began with behavioral psychology, specifically the Self-Determination Theory pioneered by Richard Ryan and Edward Deci. This fortified my conviction for academic research in the design process by offering me an authoritative reference point to develop a new system or artifact eventually integrating into existing systems.

A second phase of research sought ways to destroy pathogens in dog waste to create safe compost and propelled me towards a solution. My aim centered on a practical and scalable product that intetegrated the compost process and Austin’s public space to create a captivating and behavior-changing experience.

Vision

The solar dog waste oven appropriates a conventional solar oven to prepare dog waste for composting.

Flowering Gutters

The protruding flowering gutters hedging the wall add an element of delight to an outdoor space but also communicate the object’s use.

Poop Shoot

To keep zero contact with contaminants, a shuttle transfers the dog waste from the oven to a separate container.

Step 1

Prepare the oven by folding down the reflectors.

Step 2

Open the glass seal and place the dog waste in the container.

Step 3

Close the seal and let the dog waste cook in the sun for 1 to 2 hours.

Step 4

Remove the dog waste by sliding out the bottom plate.

Step 5

The waste will slide to the bottom and collect in a bin.

Step 6

Slide the bottom plate back in and the process is complete.

Location and Duration

The city will introduce the solar dog waste oven and composting system as a pilot program at Auditorium Shores’ dog park for several months.

Maintenance

To ensure proper cooking of the dog waste, the city will provide a grounds keeper to oversee the process but responsibility will be shared between dog owners and the grounds keeper.

Materials

An operation includes 3-15 solar dog waste ovens, bins for cooked and uncooked dog waste, bins for a carbon-rich source, frames for compost piles, shovels, and hoes.

Vertical Garden

To raise awareness about the compost program, the solar dog waste oven, and the benefits of composting, the city will plant a vertical garden.

The garden uses the compost made from the dog waste and showcases Texas wildflowers. The unusual dimension attracts people to the park as well as reveals the possibilities of integrating nature into urban areas.

Theory Explained

The theory observes people have needs for competency, autonomy, and relatedness. When supplied, well-interalized extrinsic motivation or intrinsic motivation fuels behavior. When thwarted, uninternalized or partially internalized extrinsic motivation fuels behavior change.

Application

Autonomy supporting policy dialectically engages people to consider what is right and wrong rather than controlling policies which tend to elicit defiance and resentment in people.
