Materials Lab Interface

For the UT School of Architecture’s Materials Lab

Objective

To create an interface that shows the total sustainability of building materials and lets users compare building materials.

Context

The Materials Lab maintains the largest and most comprehensive academic material collection in the world with over 27,000 material samples.

They asked for concepts for a user interface to help students and professors research and compare the properties and life cycles of building materials.

Disciplines

  • Data Visualization
  • Interaction Design
  • Information Visualization
  • User Interface Design

Skills and Processes

  • Contextual Research
  • Wireframing

Tools

  • Illustrator
  • Photoshop

Primary and Secondary Tasks

The design favors the comparison of materials primarily and the display of data as secondary.

Levels of Information

Multiple levels information compare broad cateogries of materials to specific materials made by a manufacturer.

Weekly Meetings

I met weekly with the project manager to discuss user interface concepts. We selected one particular design and I refined it several times.
