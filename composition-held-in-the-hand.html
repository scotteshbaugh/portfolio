Composition Held in the Hand

From Syntax in Visual Communication taught by David Shields

Question

To use the text and image from James Mosley’s article Type Held in the Hand to develop a composition that delivers information to the reader in an orderly, dynamic way.

Context

Mosely’s text appeared originally on his blog. As a reader the experience was cumbersome. He references images often and the blog’s format made making connections difficult. His subject was also dense and the blog’s steady pace allowed no room for rest.

My goal was to relieve future readers from this burden. The text needed space to breathe while the images needed to deliver more clarity and inspiration. Keeping this in mind, the reader also needed relief from solving which image Mosley was referring to in his text. My solution was to keep the images and the referring text on the same page.
