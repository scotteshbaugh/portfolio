Visualizing City Planning

Objective

To understand and visualize the various elements, connections, and functions within a complex system or network.

Statement

As Austin grows, problems such as traffic, affordability, gentrification, and maintaining the city’s legacy confront the city. Involving citizens helps city planners develop Austin to reflect the cultural identity of its people. Austin provides fragmented and hard to access information about city planning, stifling citizens from understanding an accurate picture of the system. Further, city planning’s complexity requires questioning, clarification, and contemplation.

The end goal was to present the system with a clear verbal and visual language an average citizen could understand. This required me to develop skills of balancing visual principles, written language, and arrange what information to include or exclude.

Form as Information

A limited selection of symbols and colors reveal relational and procedural patterns as the process moves start to finish.

Hard System Approach

Because of my target audience, I did not account the stocks and flows psychological, social, and cultural elements add to the system in real life.

A Reinterpretation

A second iteration explored nebulous forms to express the system as loosely arrayed and not bound by a set of logic and predictability.

Natural User Interface

I envisioned an interactive multi-touch table to access layers of information beneath touch points on the system map. A large screen environment lets users focus on details but remain aware of their orientation within the system.

Refinements

A third iteration found a middle ground between the first and second versions.
