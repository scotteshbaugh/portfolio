Visualizing City Planning

Project included in the 2014 Senior Design Exhibition, Make, Do, Mend

Question

How do I clarify the complexity of city planning in Austin?

Context

Every building in the city is the result of a rigorous planning process. Austinites can play an important role, but most find the process inaccessible.

While low participation is a wicked problem, this map is a starting point to address Austinites' confusion and apathy surrounding city planning.

Form as Information

A limited selection of symbols and colors reveal relational and procedural patterns as the process moves start to finish.

Hard System Approach

Because of my target audience, I did not account the stocks and flows psychological, social, and cultural elements add to the system in real life.

A Reinterpretation

A second iteration explored nebulous forms to express the system as loosely arrayed and not bound by a set of logic and predictability.

Natural User Interface

I envisioned an interactive multi-touch table to access layers of information beneath touch points on the system map. A large screen environment lets users focus on details but remain aware of their orientation within the system.

Refinements

A third iteration found a middle ground between the first and second versions.
