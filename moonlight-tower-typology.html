Moonlight Tower Typology

Objective

To create a photographic typology of designed, man-made artifacts and consider its consequences in both form and content.

Statement

Inspired by Ernst and Bernt Becker, I sought a photographic typology in a strict, rational way. As patterns emerge in typologies they create new meaning and function as a visual database. I aimed to meet this end.

Creating the images was strenuous. Each tower is six photos merged together. Controlling exposure, the frame, and distance were challenges for consistent images. The outcome, in the end, was rewarding.

As I continued I questioned the scale, arrangement, and how the viewer would engage with the photos. Solving these questions, I discovered, could be answered through an iterative process of experimentation.
