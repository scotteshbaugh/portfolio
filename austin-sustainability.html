Austin Sustainability

For the City of Austin's Office of Sustainability

Objective

To clearly organize and present data and information across for desktop devices and a multi-touch table.

Context

The City of Austin gathered data to describe the city's progress toward sustainability and sought to make their findings clear and accessible for its citizens.

They tasked me to create an interface that packaged their content in clear and compelling way. They required the design to support multiple devices and be simple enough to easily add and update content.

First Explorations

The intended audience and content were previously determined. I started off by exploring different ways to present the content.

Vertical Accordian

I thought how information could be layered and expanded to orientate users while making the content easily accessible.

Style Tile

This style tile of the main menu was used to get a gauge of the client's expectations and discuss the project.

Conceptual Mapping

The interconnected heptagon is a metaphor describing sustainable concepts as whole, balanced, and connected.

Rather than organizing information by category, I searched for ways to organize it in different forms to place emphasis on the connections between information.

User Navigation

A design challenge was to create a consistent navigation system for a large amount of content.

Choosing a Path

While blending navigation with conceptual maps is compelling, I chose UI patterns implemented with minimal javascript and CSS transitions and closer to home with users.

Additionally, for the first design I wanted it progressively enhanced.

Refining the Design

I chose against accordian menus for navigating to content.

Accordian menus hide information and force users to make decisions while exploring content. Many users will not have prior knowledge of the content and this could be overwhelming.
