You and the City

From Senior Project in Design taught by Jett Butler of FÕDA Studio

Objective

To explain zoning to Austinites to encourage participation in the city planning process.

Context

Austin is known for its unique character and amicable relationship to its natural surroundings. But in recent years, exploding growth threatens the city’s identity while an outdated zoning code baffles most residents.

My focus was to help Austinites understand how zoning intimately affects their lives and lower the entry barriers to the conversation about the future of their communities.

Process

Research was involved at every step of the process in strategizing to get more Austinites involved in city planning.

The first phase of research focused on broad research in moral psychology and land use planning. The second phase focused on zoning codes.

Mapping Values

I thought how conceptual mapping could explain the complexities of cities and relate them to land use strategies and people's values.

Considering my intended audience and the scope of the project I tightened my constraints.

Title

In light of upcoming changes to Austin's zoning code, expert interviews, and user research, I decided to create a web resource helping Austinites understand different kinds of zoning.

Refining Illustrations

I worked through several variations to remove visual clutter to make the core of the definition to become visible.
