Democratizing City Planning

From Design Systems taught by Lindsey Culpepper

Question

To build a system using modular components.

Context

As a response my research, I looked for ways to re-imagine the planning process starting with a responsive website. I viewed their dynamic layout and architecture of information as modular—a set of standardized units.

I started the task concerned with form, but my focus shifted to looking critically at the organization of information, which needed to be similar and different enough to create a rich experience for mobile, tablet, and screen devices.

This project is a component of Visualizing City Planning.

Mobile Context

Excluding images and elaborate interactions helps from marginalizing users who might otherwise avoid the site because of capped data plans and slow service.

Printed Media

Strategically placed media helps citizens know where current planning is taking place.

Process

Social Media Strategy

The site’s two goals are to educate and engage the public.

Information Architecture

The design of information anticipates the user’s desire to share their ideas for the city’s future and learn how to stay connected first before desiring to learn how city planning works.
