Authority

Objective

To use arbitrary constraints to explore the compositional possibilities of image, typography, and content and contemplate their intentional or unintentional meanings.

Statement

Rather than focusing on a specific communication goal, the project focused on working through a specific creative process. The result refined and challenged my visual literacy and approaches to problem solving.

I valued looking beyond conventional and habitual solutions of creating authoritative graphic messages. In its place, I developed a number of techniques including leveraging the embedded geometries of an image and building typographic compositions around areas of contrast to control the viewers eye, and personifying words and letters with spacial and proportional relationships.

Theoretically, the project parsed out a creative process to actively apply rather than passively and intermittently stumbling into.

Personifying Type

Entertaining myself with the spacial arrangements of type led me to think about how their placement communicated different messages and emotions.

Set of Constraints

1. Photograph images of “where something ends.” 2. Typeset posters with 5 selected typefaces. 3. Apply a series of phrases from Brian Eno’s Oblique Strategies

Finding Contrast

Rather than using scale and repetition, I realized I could also use contrast to create an authoritative message.

Embedded Geometries

I ignored a typographic grid and arranged the type to cooridinate with existing elements to negotiate a new grid.
